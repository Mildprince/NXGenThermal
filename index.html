Chip Cooling

Our system leverages and perfectly balances the power of evaporative cooling, and it’s exceptionally high heat transfer coefficient, with the unique physics of enhanced cooling though the use of proprietary microchannels. With our copper device placed directly on the chip, with heat spreader removed, all resistance from thermal pastes and other materials are eliminated. A carefully calibrated flow system optimizes the performance, avoiding burnout and boiling. It can be deployed as a new system in Data centers, as a retrofit for liquid cooling systems, or even applied to immersion cooling.

High Heat Flux Challenges

The company has developed and tested a working prototype that has delivered perfomance results similar to those of high performance liquid cooling. With the technology and science now confirmed, it is moving forward to its next gen prototype which will utilize advanced materials such as graphene, silicon, and sophisticated methods such as electrostatic etching, and be built in a clean room environment. The company’s modeling suggest that this next-gen evaporative cooling solution using advanced materials can remove 7X to 10X more heat than the traditional copper based prototype, making it ideal for AI GPUs, #D ICs, and high density data centers.
